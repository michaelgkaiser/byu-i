The Austrian-Hungarian Empire dominated Europe for half a century and became the home of many who shaped history, the world of science, music and the arts. This dominating monarchy ended with the outbreak of the first World War. The Austrian is by nature resilient and driven, and despite the ravages of two world wars and economic crisis, Austrians rebuilt their beloved home and held on to the traditions that would carry them through the years and give them a sense of pride and patriotic love for the place they call 'Oesterreich' - the kingdom in the East.

Austrians up close

Austrians are fond of their spare time and in the south-eastern provinces of Styria and Carinthia it is very popular to visit a 'Buschenschank' in the evening, a farm-to-table eating experience. Soccer is the most popular sport in the country, and even the smallest village boasts its own soccer field. As fans are fiercly loyal, games are well attended, and the excitement can be heard for miles. Snow-skiing comes in as a close second and since the country lies at the eastern edge of the Alps there is no shortage of snow in the mountains, and fathers and mothers take great pride in shepherding their offspring down the slopes.

Live like an Austrian

With its mountains, lakes, forests and fields, Austria invites you to get outside and find your favorite spot to relax and let nature wash over you. Maybe it is this ample supply of fresh air that gives Austrians a very high life expectancy, for both men and women. Traditions are passed down through the families, from the old the the next generation, and so it is well worth to catch a religious or cultural festival and buy a souvenir at an old-fashioned farmer's market.

Experience the warm welcome

There is no perfect season to visit Austria. You can expect to be received with warm hearts and open arms, and you will want to come back. If you take the time you will find that the Germanic roots with the often harsh-sounding German language seemingly gives these mountain people a hard shell, but Austrians are like the wonderful bread they bake - a crusty outside, warm and comforting on the inside.
